<p><strong>Key Programmes / Activities</strong>
</p>
<p>Physical education (PE) is an integral part of the school curriculum,
providing students with opportunities to acquire the knowledge, skills
and values that support the lifelong pursuit of an active and healthy lifestyle.
The PE programme also lays a strong foundation for students who wish to
pursue physical sports at higher levels. Together with other school programmes,
PE, Outdoor Education, and Physical Sports CCA contribute to students’
holistic development and the cultivation of emerging 21st Century Competencies
(E21CC) such as collaboration, effective communication, critical, adaptive
and inventive thinking - preparing each learner to thrive in a fast-changing
and highly connected world.</p>
<p><strong>Tiered Approach to Physical Education</strong>
</p>
<p>Recognising that every child has different physical abilities, interests
and motivations, the Department adopts a two‑tiered approach to provide
differentiated and meaningful experiences in Physical Education:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Tier 1: Broad‑based exposure and development of physical competencies
through mass participation at a recreational level</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Tier 2: Specialised training for students with interest and aptitude,
offered through Physical Sports Co‑Curricular Activities for participation
at a higher level</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through this tiered approach, every child is enabled to experience joy
in physical activity at a level that best suits their interests — whether
recreational or competitive — thereby fostering positive attitudes towards
lifelong participation in physical activities.</p>
<p>At the primary school level, students focus on developing fundamental
movement skills through the application of key movement concepts. They
build efficiency, effectiveness and versatility in movement as they practice
and transfer skills across the three learning areas of PE:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Physical Activity</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Outdoor Education</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Physical Health and Safety</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through these integrated learning experiences, students grow into confident
and competent movers who are physically literate and health‑conscious.</p>

<p><strong>Supporting Grow Well SG</strong>
</p>
<p>Our school actively supports Grow Well SG, a nationwide health initiative
that promotes healthy lifestyles in children through a whole‑school approach.
This initiative focuses on the four ‘Wells’:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Eat Well</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Sleep Well</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Learn Well</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Exercise Well</p>
</li>
</ul>
